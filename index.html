Draft Rules & Tutorial

  • Speaker Order: A higher speaker order determines your seat at the table and your order of priority when picking a strategy card.
  • Selection: Players are required to choose one of the 9 available combinations of Faction, Slice, and Speaker Order.
  • New Players: Please watch the primer below to understand the rules and victory conditions.

Naaz-Rokha Alliance

Speaker #1

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

The Naaz-Rokha are exploration specialists. They can explore a planet twice and gain Relics for only two fragments instead of three. Their versatile mechs can transform into space-combat-ready "Z-Grafts," making them formidable both on the ground and in the stars.

Federation of Sol

Speaker #2

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

Masters of logistics and infantry. Sol generates extra command tokens and can drop infantry directly onto planets from orbit without needing a transport. They are incredibly difficult to dislodge once they occupy a system.

The Ghosts of Creuss

Speaker #3

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

Mobile and elusive, the Ghosts play with the board's wormholes. They treat all wormholes as adjacent and can even move the Delta wormhole to different systems. They excel at attacking from unexpected angles across the galaxy.

Emirates of Hacan

Speaker #4

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

The merchant kings. Hacan can trade with any player, regardless of proximity, and are the only faction that can trade Action Cards. If they are in the game, the economy flows through them.

Barony of Letnev

Speaker #5

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

Industrial military might. Letnev can sustain larger fleets and spend trade goods to boost their combat rolls. They focus on building massive armadas and "Non-Euclidean Shielding" to absorb damage.

The Empyrean

Speaker #6

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

Diplomats of the void. They gain benefits when players move through their systems and can safely navigate nebulae. They thrive on negotiation and "neighborly" cooperation rather than direct conflict.

Xxcha Kingdom

Speaker #7

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

The ultimate defensive faction. Xxcha uses powerful PDS systems to fire into adjacent systems and can use their political influence to quash unwanted laws or score extra points in the late game.

Sardakk N’orr

Speaker #8

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

Raw aggressive force. The Sardakk swarm receives a constant +1 to all combat rolls. They don't start with technology, but they don't need it to win battles through sheer numerical and combat superiority.

The Last Bastion

Speaker #9

View Wiki

Map Slice

Faction Playstyle

A specialized defensive force. This faction focuses on fortifying its home slice and outlasting the competition. Expect heavy resistance if you attempt to breach their borders late in the game.
